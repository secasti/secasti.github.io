*I am a Software Engineer and GIS Specialist with a background in wildlife management and community engagement.*

I studied Wildlife Management (Conservation Biology emphasis) and Geospatial Analysis at Cal Poly Humboldt, where I made the Dean's List and fell in love with the Pacific Northwest and environmental stewardship!

 My professional journey began in environmental interpretation working for the National Park Service and [California State Parks in Downtown Los Angeles, California](https://www.instagram.com/p/B84LxNbgfe2/?hl=en). Afterwards, I pursued environmental consulting and worked with Stantec and Montrose Environmental. As an Environmental Professional, I supported large-scale compliance projects, staffed qualified biologists and monitored bird nests under contract with [Southern California Edison's vegetation management programs](https://energized.edison.com/stories/hazard-tree-removal-helps-reduce-wildfire-risk) throughout California.

Challenging myself professionally has always been a strong passion of mine and I moved to Seattle, Washington when I got accepted into the competitive [Ada Developer's Academy](https://adadevelopersacademy.org/organization/). After completing the program and an [internship with AWS Cloud Watch](/images/aws_internship.jpeg), I became a [Software Development Engineer with Amazon Retail's Competitive Pricing team](https://sellercentral.amazon.com/help/hub/reference/external/GWWTABHFJ3DMY7ZR). I engineered complex global pricing systems, maintained our pricing engine with AWS tools and managed security compliance campaigns for my team. Today, I combine my ecological knowledge with full-stack development to build tools like the various projects in [my portfolio](https://secasti.github.io/).

In my spare time [I enjoy volunteering for Latino Outdoors](https://www.instagram.com/p/CfC7XxOP41h/?hl=en) and the [Washington GIS Association](https://wagisa.org/). I'm also learning how to sew repairs and take better photos!
